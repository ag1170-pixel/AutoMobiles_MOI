A Journey Through Time

The automobile history dates back to the late 18th century. Nicolas Joseph Cugnot, a French engineer, invented the first self-propelled automobile, which used steam power for locomotion.

In the early days, the automobile industry was dominated by steam-powered vehicles. However, these vehicles were expensive, difficult to maintain, and prone to boiler explosions. The commercial history of automobiles began with the invention of gasoline-powered internal combustion engines.

1885

Karl Benz, a German inventor, constructed his first gasoline-powered vehicle.

1888

Commercial production of Benz cars started.

The early 1900s saw the rise of many automobile manufacturing companies in Europe and the United States. The first mass-produced automobile in the US was the curved-dash Oldsmobile. Henry Ford's Model T car disrupted the US car market, selling a million units by 1920.

Mass production made cars more affordable, leading to a revolution in automobile manufacturing. The British industry was transformed by assembly line production methods. The 1960s saw rapid developments in technology, including fuel injection, independent suspensions, and turbochargers.
