Cars in India

The Indian Car market is on a high, it is said to be free from the pangs of global economic meltdown. The Indian economy is on a steady rise with the rise of the per capita incomes of Indians. The country is among the emerging economies with a stable growth rate of 5% annually. The Indian car market which grew from 1.77 million units in 2008 to 1.83 million units in 2009, promises to do even better with a forecasted growth of 3 million units in the year 2015. The Indian car market has realized the demand trend and is manufacturing cars accordingly. Car sales in India witnessed an increase rate of 17% from 2003 - 2008. Since India is emerged as one of the largest producers of small cars due to its strong engineering base and cost effective labor the figures of its exports are also rising at an alarming rate.

The car market in India will witness a major expansion in the mid size B segment and the small car segment will also grow at an alarming rate as well. Initially India was only considered to be a market for small cars but with the success of cars such as the Honda City, Toyota Corolla and the Ford Fiesta, India is becoming a favorable market for international automobile giants. Every car manufactured in India today is either made specifically for the Indian consumer or is a niche car, which is craved for globally. India is no longer a technologically backward nation with technologies such as CRDi, ABS, ESP, climate control, adaptive dampers, ride control, the Indian car manufacturers are all geared up to give their foreign competitors a tough competition. Gone are the days when cars were considered a luxury in India today cars are becoming a necessity
